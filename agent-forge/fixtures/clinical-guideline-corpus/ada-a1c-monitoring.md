## A1c Monitoring Frequency

For patients with diabetes who are meeting treatment goals and have stable glycemic control, A1c is commonly reviewed at least two times per year.

## A1c Therapy Change Follow-Up

For patients whose therapy has changed or who are not meeting glycemic goals, A1c is commonly reviewed quarterly until control is stable.

## A1c Follow-Up Context

An elevated A1c should be interpreted with patient-specific factors, medication adherence, hypoglycemia risk, comorbidities, and current treatment plan.

## A1c Evidence Boundary

This excerpt supports follow-up context for A1c monitoring. It does not authorize patient-specific diagnosis, medication changes, or urgent triage without clinician review.
